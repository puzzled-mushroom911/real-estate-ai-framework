# Content & Brand Voice Rules

> All content work serves {{AGENT_NAME}}'s real estate business and should sound like them -- learned from their own content and data.

## Hook Formula
- Direct hook + promise + filter + curiosity element
- Pattern: Unique fact/pain point --> "You need to know what you're getting into" --> Roadmap of coverage --> Credibility line
- Credibility line: "{{AGENT_CREDENTIAL_LINE}}" -- customize per agent but keep it specific and provable
- Example (Pros & Cons): Lead with popularity + scarcity ("most popular relocation destination... but most people don't realize how limited your options are")
- Keep hooks viewer-focused, not overly emotional/personal about the agent
- Save personal stories for later in the content where they fit naturally as proof points

## CON-to-PRO Structure
- Acknowledge the negative first, then flip it
- This builds trust -- audiences feel heard, then get the balanced perspective
- Example: "Yes, insurance rates jumped 40% last year. But here's what that means for buyers right now..."
- Never dismiss cons or sugarcoat -- audiences detect it instantly

## Content Positioning
- NEVER frame content value around "nobody else is covering this" or "zero YouTube videos on this topic"
- The brand stands on its own merit, not on being first
- Content value comes from {{AGENT_NAME}}'s local expertise, personal experience, and honest analysis
- Specific data over vague claims -- always cite numbers, dates, or sources when available
- No generic realtor language ("dream home," "perfect for families," "turnkey," "motivated seller")

## Voice Guidelines
- Direct, conversational -- every sentence earns its space
- Specific data over vague claims
- Honest about cons -- this IS the trust mechanism
- {{AGENT_TEAM_DESCRIPTION}} perspective (e.g., "husband-wife team," "solo agent," etc.)
- Voice should be matched from the agent's ingested content (videos, podcasts, reviews, website copy, voice notes)

## Credibility Rules
- Use the agent's verified credential line: "{{AGENT_CREDENTIAL_LINE}}"
- Never inflate numbers or make unverifiable claims
- Use "buyers" not "families" -- avoid steering or showing prejudice toward family status (Fair Housing)
- Never use "family home" as a persona label -- use "large home" or describe by bedrooms/sqft
- Keep wording authentic and general

## Content Types & Priorities
1. **Original content** (videos, podcasts, blog posts) -- the voice source and lead engine
2. **Repurposed content** (blog posts from transcripts, emails, social clips) -- multiplier effect
3. **Lead magnets** (guides, neighborhood comparison PDFs, market reports) -- conversion tools
4. **Nurture sequences** (emails, SMS follow-ups) -- keep leads warm in the agent's voice

## CTA Guidelines
- Mid-content CTA: soft, value-forward ("I put together a free guide on this...")
- End-content CTA: direct but not pushy ("If you're seriously considering {{AGENT_CITY}}, let's talk")
- Always include booking link: {{AGENT_BOOKING_LINK}}
- Never use high-pressure sales language in content
