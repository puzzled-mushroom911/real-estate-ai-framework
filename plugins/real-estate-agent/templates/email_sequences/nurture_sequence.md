# Weekly Nurture Email Framework

## Overview
Ongoing weekly emails for leads who've completed the welcome sequence. Goal: stay top-of-mind, build trust, and move them toward a conversation when they're ready.

## Weekly Schedule

### Monday: Weekend Observation
Something you noticed over the weekend while out in the market.
- "So I was at [place] Saturday and..."
- "Three open houses this weekend, and all three had the same problem..."
- Keep it observational, not salesy

### Tuesday: Neighborhood Spotlight
One specific area, with real details.
- Name the streets, cite prices, describe the vibe
- Include one CON and flip it to a PRO
- "If you're the type who [lifestyle], this is your spot"

### Wednesday: Honest CON-to-PRO
Address a real downside of your market and reframe it.
- Insurance costs, traffic, heat, tourist crowds, flood zones
- "Yes, [con] is real. But here's what most people miss..."
- Builds trust through transparency

### Thursday: Client Pattern or Market Data
Share something data-driven or pattern-based.
- "3 out of my last 5 buyers ended up in [area] — here's why"
- "Median price just hit [X] in [area]. Here's what that actually means for buyers"
- Always translate the data into what it means for THEM

### Friday: Lifestyle Pick
A restaurant, activity, event, or local spot worth knowing about.
- "Aubrey's pick this week: [place]"
- Or: "[Event] is coming up on [date] — here's why it matters if you're new here"
- Light, personal, shows you actually live there

## Seasonal Specials
Replace a regular email when timely:
- **Hurricane season** (June): Prep tips, insurance reminders, flood zone explainer
- **Snowbird season** (Oct-Nov): Traffic patterns, seasonal pricing shifts
- **Summer** (Jul-Aug): Heat survival guide, indoor activities, school enrollment deadlines
- **Tax season** (Jan-Mar): Homestead exemption reminders, property tax tips
- **Holiday** (Dec): Year-end market recap, best holiday events in your area

## Rules
- Never send more than 3 emails per week
- Always include phone number in signature
- Never pressure — educate and invite
- Rotate between story-driven and data-driven content
- If a lead replies, switch to personal 1:1 conversation immediately
