# Welcome Email Sequence Template

## Overview
5 emails over 10 days for new subscribers (typically YouTube viewers who downloaded a guide or booked a call).

---

## Email 1 - Day 1: The Origin Story
**Subject options:**
- "here's why I do this differently"
- "quick story about how I got into real estate"
- "the thing that changed everything for me"

**Structure:**
- Opening: Jump into your story (why you became an agent, or why you focus on relocation)
- Story: 100-word version of your origin — what happened, the struggle, the realization
- Pivot: "That's why I built [your approach/brand] — because [lesson from story]"
- CTA: "Reply and tell me where you're moving from. I read every single one."
- P.S.: Quick local fact about your area

---

## Email 2 - Day 3: The #1 Mistake
**Subject options:**
- "the biggest mistake I see [city] buyers make"
- "don't do this when moving to [state]"
- "I wish someone had told me this"

**Structure:**
- Opening: "Almost every buyer I work with makes the same mistake in the first week..."
- Story: A real (anonymized) client example
- The lesson: What they should do instead
- CTA: "Want my [neighborhood guide / market report]? Just reply 'guide' and I'll send it."
- P.S.: Seasonal tip or upcoming event

---

## Email 3 - Day 5: The Hidden Gem
**Subject options:**
- "the neighborhood nobody talks about"
- "my favorite spot in [city] — and it's not [obvious area]"
- "drove through here yesterday and had to share"

**Structure:**
- Opening: Start mid-drive or mid-showing
- Story: Paint the picture of a specific neighborhood
- What makes it special: Specific details (street names, price ranges, vibe)
- CTA: "I've got a full breakdown of [area] neighborhoods. Want it?"
- P.S.: Restaurant or activity recommendation in that area

---

## Email 4 - Day 7: The Pattern
**Subject options:**
- "after helping [X]+ buyers, I noticed something"
- "here's what most of my clients end up choosing"
- "the [city] buying pattern nobody expects"

**Structure:**
- Opening: "After helping [number] buyers relocate here, I started noticing a pattern..."
- Insight: What most buyers think they want vs. what they actually choose
- Why: The real reason (usually a practical one — commute, schools, lifestyle fit)
- CTA: "Curious which area might be right for you? I've got a 5-question quiz — reply 'quiz'"
- P.S.: Market stat that supports the pattern

---

## Email 5 - Day 10: The Direct Value
**Subject options:**
- "free [resource] for your [city] move"
- "I put this together for you"
- "everything I know about [area], in one doc"

**Structure:**
- Opening: Short, direct — "I put together something I think will help."
- Value: Describe the resource (guide, market report, neighborhood comparison)
- Why it's different: "This isn't generic internet stuff — it's from my actual experience helping buyers on the ground here."
- CTA: Direct link or "reply for a copy"
- P.S.: "And if you want to just hop on a quick call, here's my calendar: [booking link]"

---

## Notes
- Each email should be under 200 words
- Use plain text formatting (no HTML graphics)
- Send from a personal email address, not a marketing platform address
- Subject lines should look like a text, not a newsletter
